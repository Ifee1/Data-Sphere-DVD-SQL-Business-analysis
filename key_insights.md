This section summarizes the key business insights derived from the SQL analysis of the Maven Movies database.
---
## 1. Store Inventory Distribution
Inventory across the stores shows a relatively balanced distribution of film ratings.  
However, certain ratings appear more frequently in specific stores, suggesting localized customer preferences.

**Business Implication:**  
Stores may be tailoring inventory to match customer demographics or viewing preferences in their region.

---
## 2. Replacement Cost Exposure
A significant portion of inventory replacement cost is concentrated within a few film categories.

**Business Implication:**  
If customer demand shifts away from these categories, the business could face higher financial exposure due to replacement costs.

**Recommendation:**  
Diversifying inventory categories across stores could help reduce financial risk.

---

## 3. Customer Value Concentration
Customer spending is unevenly distributed, with a small group of customers contributing a large share of total revenue.

**Business Implication:**  
High-value customers are critical to the business and should be prioritized through loyalty programs or targeted promotions.
---

## 4. Customer Activity Levels
Customer records include both active and inactive users across stores.

**Business Implication:**  
Inactive customers represent an opportunity for re-engagement strategies such as promotional campaigns or targeted communication.
---

## 5. Film Catalog Coverage of Award-Winning Actors
The catalog includes films featuring many award-winning actors, particularly those with multiple awards.

**Business Implication:**  
Maintaining a strong presence of highly recognized actors may help attract customers seeking popular or critically acclaimed films.
